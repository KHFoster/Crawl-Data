The SFO is opening its 60th season on Friday, July 1.</p> <div class="p402_hide"><div id="attachment_797954" style="width: 410px" class="wp-caption alignleft"><a href="http://www.abqjournal.com/?attachment_id=797954" rel="attachment wp-att-797954"><img class="size-medium wp-image-797954" src="http://main.abqjournal.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/06/24/life03_jd_26jun_4singer-400x272.jpg" alt="Regina Sarfaty, center, singing the title role in “Carmen” in 1961." width="400" height="272" srcset="http://main.abqjournal.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/06/24/life03_jd_26jun_4singer-400x272.jpg 400w, http://main.abqjournal.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/06/24/life03_jd_26jun_4singer-175x119.jpg 175w, http://main.abqjournal.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/06/24/life03_jd_26jun_4singer-768x522.jpg 768w, http://main.abqjournal.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/06/24/life03_jd_26jun_4singer-640x435.jpg 640w, http://main.abqjournal.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/06/24/life03_jd_26jun_4singer-100x68.jpg 100w, http://main.abqjournal.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/06/24/life03_jd_26jun_4singer-150x102.jpg 150w, http://main.abqjournal.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/06/24/life03_jd_26jun_4singer-250x170.jpg 250w, http://main.abqjournal.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/06/24/life03_jd_26jun_4singer-350x238.jpg 350w, http://main.abqjournal.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/06/24/life03_jd_26jun_4singer-500x340.jpg 500w, http://main.abqjournal.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/06/24/life03_jd_26jun_4singer-700x475.jpg 700w" sizes="(max-width: 400px) 100vw, 400px"></a><p class="wp-caption-text">Regina Sarfaty, center, singing the title role in “Carmen” in 1961.</p></div></div><p><b>In the beginning</b></p><p>Sarfaty met Crosby at New York’s Juilliard School when she was a student and he was the rehearsal pianist. She remembers a quiet, polite man who spoke with his head bowed. He was just 30.</p><p>“He said, ‘I’m going to start an opera company in Santa Fe,’ ” she said. “I said, ‘Where’s Santa Fe?’ ”</p><p>Crosby was the son of an attorney and a professional violinist based in Bronxville, N.Y. He first came to New Mexico through the Los Alamos Ranch School in 1940.</p><p>Doctors had advised his parents to send him West to a drier climate because of his chronic sinusitis. He thrived in Los Alamos, took up sports, and returned home the next year.</p><div class="p402_hide story-ad-wrap dfp-ad alt"><p class="disclaimer">Advertisement</p><p class="ad-skipper"><a href="#ad-skipper-1">Continue reading</a></p><div id="story-ad-1" class="story-ad"><div id="google_ads_iframe_/3828090/sidebar-top_1__container__" style="border: 0pt none;"><iframe id="google_ads_iframe_/3828090/sidebar-top_1" title="3rd party ad content" name="google_ads_iframe_/3828090/sidebar-top_1" width="300" height="250" scrolling="no" marginwidth="0" marginheight="0" frameborder="0" src="javascript:&quot;<html><body style='background:transparent'></body></html>&quot;" style="border: 0px; vertical-align: bottom;"></iframe></div><iframe id="google_ads_iframe_/3828090/sidebar-top_1__hidden__" title="" name="google_ads_iframe_/3828090/sidebar-top_1__hidden__" width="0" height="0" scrolling="no" marginwidth="0" marginheight="0" frameborder="0" src="javascript:&quot;<html><body style='background:transparent'></body></html>&quot;" style="border: 0px; vertical-align: bottom; visibility: hidden; display: none;"></iframe></div><div id="ad-skipper-1" class="ad-skipper-anchor"></div></div><p>He kept coming back, first to wrangle at Santa Fe’s Bishop’s Lodge, founded in the 1860s as a retreat by Archbishop of Santa Fe Jean Baptiste Lamy. In 1948, the family bought 47 acres on Santa Fe’s Tano Road, south of what would become the Santa Fe Opera complex. It became a family magnet.</p><p>At first, Crosby considered placing his opera in more heavily populated parts of the country, including Connecticut and Maine. But no place could match the glorious summer sunsets and vistas of Santa Fe.</p><p>As a fledgling conductor, Crosby also realized Santa Fe was far from the pens of East Coast critics.</p><p>Sarfaty told Crosby to return when he had a contract and forgot about it.</p><p>When she was still a teenager, Sarfaty auditioned for a $500 Juilliard scholarship after her sister invited her into a local choral group. The daughter of a Brooklyn cantor, Sarfaty had never envisioned a singing career until all the teachers at the audition wanted her in their classes.</p><p>“I knew nobody when I started,” Sarfaty said. The pianist “Van Cliburn sat next to me in my theory class.”</p><p>The great soprano Leontyne Price was another voice student.</p><p>Crosby returned with a contract about four months after their first discussion. Sarfaty would earn $100 a week, plus room and board at the property’s old ranch house. With no other job offers, she accepted.</p><div class="p402_hide story-ad-wrap dfp-ad"><p class="disclaimer">Advertisement</p><p class="ad-skipper"><a href="#ad-skipper-2">Continue reading</a></p><div id="story-ad-2" class="story-ad"><div id="google_ads_iframe_/3828090/sidebar-top_2__container__" style="border: 0pt none;"><iframe id="google_ads_iframe_/3828090/sidebar-top_2" title="3rd party ad content" name="google_ads_iframe_/3828090/sidebar-top_2" width="300" height="250" scrolling="no" marginwidth="0" marginheight="0" frameborder="0" src="javascript:&quot;<html><body style='background:transparent'></body></html>&quot;" style="border: 0px; vertical-align: bottom;"></iframe></div></div><div id="ad-skipper-2" class="ad-skipper-anchor"></div></div><p>Crosby carefully placed his opera on a former pinto bean farm known as the San Juan Ranch. Its ridged landscape produced an ideal acoustical bowl, and its sweeping views of the Sangre de Cristo and Jemez mountains could double as scenery.</p><p>He had already begun construction by 1956. The original outdoor theater seated 500 patrons on folding chairs and wooden benches facing a covered stage with redwood walls. The swooping roof extended over the orchestra pit and a reflecting pool to boost the acoustics. There were just two dressing rooms. The old ranch house doubled as the singers’ lodging. Crosby planted white petunias in front of the theater so they could be seen at night.</p><p>Sarfaty was just 22. At first, the elevation and dryness proved a dangerous recipe, cracking her lips and shortening her breath. United Press International said, “There was nothing amateur about it.” Time magazine wondered if Santa Fe would become “the Salzburg of the Southwest.”</p><p>“Lawrence Crosby (John Crosby’s father) came up to John and said, ‘John, you have a winner here. Stravinsky’s appearance brought international attention and legitimacy to Crosby’s venture; the Santa Fe production brought “The Rake” a second life.</p><p>Sarfaty sang the role of Baba the Turk, a bearded circus performer. Her costume of low-slung harem pants crowned by a bejeweled navel was considered risqué for the Eisenhower era. To add to the pressure, Stravinsky sat in on each rehearsal and performance.</p><p>Sarfaty took an instant liking to the Russian composer.</p><p>“You couldn’t have met a nicer man in your life,” she said. “He signed my score.
